The standard or piano accordion bass, known as the Stradella system, is presented below. The board can have as few as 8 and as many as 120 buttons. The columns are increment in 5th, starting from C in the middle. The rows correspond to notes and chords. The root row is either the second from top. The rows underneath are chord rows, and are usually major, minor, 7th and diminished chords. The top row, if not the root, is the "counter-bass" row, a major 3rd up from the root. You can select all the common board types below. If sound is enabled, clicking will play the corresponding button. The board may also be viewed vertically.
